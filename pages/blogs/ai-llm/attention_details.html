1️⃣ Scaled Dot-Product Attention core

Let sequence length be n.

Query, Key, Value matrices:

\[ Q \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times d_k} \] \[ K \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times d_k} \] \[ V \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times d_v} \]

Similarity Scores

\[ S = \frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}} \]

where

\[ S \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n} \]

Attention Weights (row-wise softmax)

\[ A = \text{softmax}(S) \]

Each row sums to 1:

\[ \sum_j A_{ij} = 1 \]

Output

\[ O = AV \] \[ O \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times d_v} \]

Compact Form

\[ \text{Attention}(Q,K,V) = \text{softmax}\left( \frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}} \right)V \]

2️⃣ Token-wise View intuition

For token \( i \):

Score

\[ s_{ij} = \frac{q_i \cdot k_j}{\sqrt{d_k}} \]

Weight

\[ \alpha_{ij} = \frac{\exp(s_{ij})} {\sum_m \exp(s_{im})} \]

Output

\[ o_i = \sum_j \alpha_{ij} v_j \]

3️⃣ Why Divide by \( \sqrt{d_k} \) stability

Key idea: the dot product \(q \cdot k\) grows in magnitude as dimension grows. Large scores make softmax too peaky, which hurts gradients.

Variance scaling (the main reason)

Assume (roughly) each component of \(q\) and \(k\) is zero-mean with variance 1, and coordinates are weakly independent.

\[ q \cdot k = \sum_{r=1}^{d_k} q_r k_r \]

Each product \(q_r k_r\) has mean \(\approx 0\) and variance \(\approx 1\). Summing \(d_k\) such terms gives:

\[ \mathrm{Var}(q \cdot k) \approx d_k \qquad \Rightarrow \qquad \mathrm{Std}(q \cdot k) \approx \sqrt{d_k} \]

So without scaling, typical score magnitudes increase like \(\sqrt{d_k}\).

Softmax saturation (why this is bad)

Softmax is sensitive to the scale of its inputs. If scores are large, softmax becomes almost one-hot:

\[ \alpha_{ij} = \frac{e^{s_{ij}}}{\sum_m e^{s_{im}}} \]

When \(s_{ij}\) are large in magnitude, one entry dominates, others go near 0. Then gradients through softmax become tiny for most positions, making learning unstable or slow.

What scaling fixes

Scaling by \(\sqrt{d_k}\) normalizes the typical magnitude:

\[ s_{ij} = \frac{q_i \cdot k_j}{\sqrt{d_k}} \] \[ \mathrm{Var}(s_{ij}) \approx \frac{d_k}{d_k} = 1 \]

This keeps score magnitudes roughly dimension-invariant, preventing softmax from becoming overly sharp just because \(d_k\) is large.

Practical intuition: if \(d_k = 512\), then \(\sqrt{d_k} \approx 22.6\). Without scaling, score magnitudes can be ~20× larger, making attention weights much more extreme.

4️⃣ Multi-Head Attention representation

For head \( h \):

\[ Q_h = Q W_h^Q \] \[ K_h = K W_h^K \] \[ V_h = V W_h^V \] \[ \text{head}_h = \text{Attention}(Q_h, K_h, V_h) \]

Concatenate:

\[ \text{MultiHead}(Q,K,V) = \text{Concat}(\text{head}_1, \dots, \text{head}_H) W^O \]

5️⃣ Positional Encoding order

Why needed: self-attention is permutation-equivariant. Without position information, the model cannot distinguish “A then B” from “B then A”.

Sinusoidal positional encoding (Transformer original)

For position \(pos\) and dimension index \(i\):

\[ \mathrm{PE}(pos, 2i) = \sin\!\left(\frac{pos}{10000^{2i/d_{\mathrm{model}}}}\right) \] \[ \mathrm{PE}(pos, 2i+1) = \cos\!\left(\frac{pos}{10000^{2i/d_{\mathrm{model}}}}\right) \]

The input embedding becomes:

\[ x_{pos} = \mathrm{Embed}(token_{pos}) + \mathrm{PE}(pos) \]

What “10000” is doing

\(10000\) is a base that creates a log-spaced range of wavelengths across dimensions.

Define the wavelength for dimension \(i\) (ignoring constants like \(2\pi\)):

\[ \lambda_i \propto 10000^{2i/d_{\mathrm{model}}} \]

So small \(i\) gives short wavelengths (captures local order), while large \(i\) gives long wavelengths (captures long-range position). This makes position encoding multi-scale.

Why sin/cos (useful property)

Sinusoids allow relative position information to be represented via linear combinations, because:

\[ \sin(a+b) = \sin a \cos b + \cos a \sin b \qquad \cos(a+b) = \cos a \cos b - \sin a \sin b \]

This helps attention layers learn to reason about offsets like \(pos - pos'\) using simple linear operations.

Modern variants (context)

Many LLMs use learned positional embeddings or rotary positional embeddings (RoPE), which encode position directly in the attention computation and often extrapolate better to longer contexts.

6️⃣ Causal Attention autoregressive

Mask matrix \( M \):

\[ M_{ij} = \begin{cases} 0 & j \le i \\ -\infty & j > i \end{cases} \] \[ S = \frac{QK^T + M}{\sqrt{d_k}} \]

Ensures autoregressive property:

\[ p(x_t \mid x_{\lt t}) \]

🧠 One-Line Mental Model

Attention = softmax-normalized similarity scores + weighted aggregation of values.
